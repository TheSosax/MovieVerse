Film Ratings

Movie rating systems, like the one created by the Motion Picture Association (MPA), provide guidance on the appropriate age for viewing films. These classifications help parents make informed decisions based on content such as violence, sexual content, language, and drug use.

How It Works:

1. Evaluation

A board of parents or professionals watches the film and evaluates its content in detail.

2. Factors Considered

They look at several factors:

  • Violence: Frequency, intensity, and graphic nature.
  • Sex & Nudity: Level and context of sexual content.
  • Language: Use and severity of offensive terms.
  • Drug Use: How substances are portrayed or consumed.

3. Rating Assignment

Based on the content, the film receives a rating that reflects the appropriate viewer age.

4. Parental Guidance

Ratings act as general guidance, but parents are encouraged to review detailed content for PG or PG-13 movies.

5. Additional Information

Some organizations, like Common Sense Media, provide deeper insights on specific scenes and age-suitability.

Examples of Ratings:

G – General Audiences

Suitable for all ages. No offensive content.

PG – Parental Guidance Suggested

Some content might not be suitable for young children.

PG-13 – Parents Strongly Cautioned

Some material may be inappropriate for children under 13.

R – Restricted

Under 17 requires accompanying parent or guardian.

NC-17 – Adults Only

No one 17 and under admitted. For mature audiences only.

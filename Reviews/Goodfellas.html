Goodfellas

1990 | 2h 26m | R

Martin Scorsese's Goodfellas is a gripping and stylized journey through the rise and fall of mob associate Henry Hill. Based on true events, this film delivers unforgettable performances, sharp writing, and a brutally honest look into the American Mafia. A modern classic in gangster cinema.

“A triumphant, brutal, and electrifying depiction of organized crime.” – Peter Travers, Rolling Stone

“The best mob movie ever made — it even makes The Godfather look tame.” – Roger Ebert

“Scorsese’s masterpiece. Raw, relentless, and unforgettable.” – The Guardian

Parents Must Know

This film contains frequent and intense scenes of violence, drug use, and strong language. It portrays a realistic and often disturbing depiction of organized crime, including murder, addiction, and corruption. Parents should be aware that this movie is not suitable for younger audiences and is intended for mature viewers only.

Content Rating (+18)

  • Sex & Nudity: Moderate
  • Violence & Gore: Severe
  • Profanity: Severe
  • Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking: Severe
  • Frightening & Intense Scenes: Severe

Sex & Nudity

Several sexual situations shown or implied, though no explicit nudity is shown. Strong innuendo and suggestive content.

Violence & Gore

Graphic and realistic violence including beatings, shootings, and stabbings. Often disturbing and brutal.

Profanity

Very frequent use of strong language throughout, including racial and homophobic slurs.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Constant depictions of cocaine use, heavy drinking, and smoking. Drug trade and addiction are central to the plot.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Intense scenes of paranoia, betrayal, and brutal retribution. The chaotic lifestyle and descent into madness are deeply unsettling.

The Godfather: Part III

1990 | 2h 50m | R

The Godfather: Part III continues the epic saga of the Corleone family, focusing on Michael Corleone’s attempt to legitimize his empire and find redemption. Despite his efforts, Michael is drawn back into the violent world of organized crime. The film explores themes of guilt, legacy, and the inescapable consequences of a dark past.

“A compelling, tragic conclusion to the greatest cinematic trilogy ever.” – The New York Times

“Coppola brings closure with emotional weight and operatic intensity.” – Chicago Sun-Times

“Pacino delivers a powerful performance in this haunting final chapter.” – Empire

Parents Must Know

This film contains graphic violence including shootings, stabbings, and disturbing deaths. Moderate language is used throughout, with several profanities and some racial slurs. There is mild sexual content including implied intimacy and brief nudity. Characters are seen drinking and smoking casually. The story is emotionally intense and includes strong themes of regret, redemption, and mortality. Suitable only for mature audiences (17+).

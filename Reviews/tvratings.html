TV Show Ratings

TV show ratings measure how many people are watching a particular show and what kind of audience is tuning in. These ratings help networks, advertisers, and creators make key decisions about content and scheduling.

How TV Ratings Work:

Measurement

Ratings are gathered through statistical sampling. Nielsen Media Research uses devices called People Meters, which are installed in selected households to represent the broader viewing population. This data is not a full count of every viewer but a representative estimate.

TV Parental Guidelines: Age-Based Ratings

What Are TV Parental Guidelines?

The TV Parental Guidelines are a content rating system developed to help parents choose appropriate programming for children and teens. Each rating reflects the age group for which the content is deemed suitable, and whether it may contain violence, language, sexual content, or other mature themes.

Age Ratings and Their Meaning

  • TV-Y: Suitable for all children. Programming is designed to be appropriate for young audiences.
  • TV-Y7: For children age 7 and older. May contain mild fantasy violence. The FV label indicates more intense fantasy violence.
  • TV-G: General audience. Suitable for all ages with little or no objectionable content.
  • TV-PG: Parental guidance suggested. Some material may not be suitable for children.
  • TV-14: Parents strongly cautioned. May include strong language, sexual content, or intense violence. Not recommended for children under 14.
  • TV-MA: Mature audiences only. Content may be unsuitable for viewers under 17 and can include graphic language, explicit sexual content, or violence.

Content Descriptors

Some ratings come with content letters that specify the type of material included:

  • D: Suggestive Dialogue
  • L: Coarse or Crude Language
  • S: Sexual Content
  • V: Violence
  • FV: Fantasy Violence (only used with TV-Y7)

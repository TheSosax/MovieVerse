The Godfather

1972 | 2h 55m | R

Francis Ford Coppola's The Godfather is a cinematic masterpiece that explores the complex dynamics of power, loyalty, and family within the American Mafia. Featuring unforgettable performances from Marlon Brando and Al Pacino, it remains one of the most influential films in cinema history.

“One of the greatest movies ever made. A landmark of American cinema.” – Roger Ebert

“Brando delivers an iconic performance in a deeply atmospheric and unforgettable saga.” – The New York Times

“A timeless epic of crime, power, and tradition.” – Empire Magazine

Parents Must Know

This film contains pervasive and graphic violence, including close-range shootings, strangulation, and a car explosion. There is a memorable scene of a man waking up to a decapitated horse head in his bed. Domestic violence is depicted, and there are multiple instances of nudity and sexual innuendo. Ethnic and racial slurs are used throughout, including an extremely offensive racial slur. Alcohol and tobacco use are frequent. The movie is intense and not suitable for children.

Content Rating (17+)

  • Sex & Nudity: Moderate
  • Violence & Gore: Severe
  • Profanity: Moderate
  • Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking: Mild
  • Frightening & Intense Scenes: Moderate

Sex & Nudity

Includes brief female frontal nudity, implied sex, audible sounds, and sexual discussions during a wedding. Some sexually suggestive content involving mistresses.

Violence & Gore

Graphic mob violence including shootings, strangulation, beatings, and a decapitated horse's head. Domestic abuse and sexual assault are referenced.

Profanity

Includes racial, ethnic, and homophobic slurs. Several strong expletives and crude language appear throughout the film.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Frequent scenes of drinking and smoking. Some references to marijuana and heroin, but not graphically shown.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Includes scenes of intense emotional tension and disturbing moments, including betrayal and murder.

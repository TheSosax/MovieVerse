Kill Bill: Vol.1

2003 | 1h 51m | R

Kill Bill Vol. 1 is Quentin Tarantino’s stylish revenge epic, following The Bride (Uma Thurman), a former assassin left for dead by her own team. Awakening from a coma, she embarks on a brutal journey to eliminate those who betrayed her. With breathtaking action, stunning cinematography, and a mix of martial arts and Western influences, Kill Bill Vol. 1 is a visually striking and adrenaline-fueled ride.

“A stunningly choreographed symphony of violence.” – Empire

“Tarantino at his most stylish and unrestrained.” – Roger Ebert

“A cinematic bloodbath with elegance and flair.” – The New York Times

Parents Must Know

Parents must know: Kill Bill Vol. 1 is rated R, meaning it contains strong bloody violence, language, and some sexual content. The film features intense fight sequences, sword combat, and stylized gore. There are moments of profanity and references to sexual assault, though no explicit nudity is shown1. The violence is exaggerated and often cartoonish, but still graphic.

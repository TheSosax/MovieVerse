Schindler's List

1993 | 3h 15m | R

Schindler's List (1993), directed by Steven Spielberg, tells the true story of Oskar Schindler, a German businessman who saved over a thousand Jewish lives during the Holocaust by employing them in his factory. Set in Nazi-occupied Poland, the film explores themes of humanity, morality, and resistance in the face of unimaginable horror. Starring Liam Neeson, Ben Kingsley, and Ralph Fiennes, it is widely regarded as one of the most powerful films ever made.

Parents Must Know

This film contains graphic depictions of violence, nudity, and emotionally intense scenes related to the Holocaust. It portrays genocide, persecution, and moral dilemmas with stark realism. Due to its mature content and historical weight, it is recommended only for older teens and adults capable of processing its themes.

Content Rating (+18)

  • Sex & Nudity: severe
  • Violence & Gore: severe
  • Profanity: moderate

  • Frightening & Intense Scenes: severe

Sex & Nudity

Includes scenes of full frontal nudity in both sexual and non-sexual contexts. Sexual harassment and assault are depicted. Nudity is also shown during medical inspections and public showers in concentration camps.

Violence & Gore

Frequent and graphic depictions of shootings, beatings, and executions. Scenes of genocide, torture, and mass graves are shown with disturbing realism.

Profanity

Moderate use of strong language including the F-word and anti-Semitic slurs. Profanity is present but not excessive.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Characters are frequently seen drinking and smoking. Some individuals appear intoxicated or are implied to be alcoholics.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Emotionally devastating scenes of persecution, despair, and death. The film’s tone is relentlessly intense and may be overwhelming for sensitive viewers.

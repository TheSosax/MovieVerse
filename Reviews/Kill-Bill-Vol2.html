Kill Bill: Vol.2

2004 | 2h 17m | R

Kill Bill Vol. 2 shifts from the high-energy action of the first film to a more introspective and emotional journey. The Bride (Uma Thurman) continues her quest for revenge, facing off against her remaining enemies, including the enigmatic Bill (David Carradine). With deeper character development, intense confrontations, and Tarantino’s signature dialogue, Kill Bill Vol. 2 delivers a powerful conclusion to the saga.

“A revenge tale with unexpected soul.” – A. O. Scott, The New York Times

“Tarantino blends violence and poetry into a visual symphony.” – Roger Ebert

“More introspective than its predecessor, yet equally intense.” – Peter Travers, Rolling Stone

Parents Must Know

Parents must know: Kill Bill Vol. 2 is rated R, meaning it contains strong violence, language, and brief drug use. While less gory than the first film, it still features intense fight scenes, emotional confrontations, and moments of brutality. There is some profanity, references to drug use, and a scene set in a strip club. The film’s themes of vengeance and survival make it a mature viewing experience2.

F1: The Movie

2025 | 2h 35m | PG-13

F1: The Movie (2025), directed by Joseph Kosinski, stars Brad Pitt as Sonny Hayes, a retired Formula One driver who returns to the sport to mentor a young rookie, Joshua Pearce (Damson Idris). With real F1 teams and drivers appearing, the film blends high-octane racing with emotional depth. The cast includes Kerry Condon, Javier Bardem, and Tobias Menzies, with Lewis Hamilton as producer.

“Brad Pitt brings vintage cool across the finish line.” – Rotten Tomatoes

“A turbo-charged Dad Movie par excellence.” – Little White Lies

“Visually immersive, emotionally grounded.” – Roger Ebert

Parents Must Know

This film contains intense racing sequences, moderate profanity, and mature themes such as trauma, redemption, and mentorship. While sexual content is minimal, the emotional tone and crash scenes may be too intense for younger viewers. Recommended for ages 13 and up.

Content Rating (PG-13)

  • Sex & Nudity: mild
  • Violence & Gore: mild
  • Profanity: moderate
  • Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking: mild
  • Frightening & Intense Scenes: moderate

Sex & Nudity

A couple kiss passionately; sexual activity is implied but not shown. No nudity.

Violence & Gore

Racing crashes, fiery explosions, and intense sequences. One driver is pulled from a burning car. No graphic injuries.

Profanity

Several uses of strong language including the F-word (twice), and other profanities like “s–t” and “a–.”

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Characters drink beer and spirits in social settings. One character smokes briefly. No drug use.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Flashbacks of a traumatic crash, intense racing scenes, and emotional confrontations. May be distressing for sensitive viewers.

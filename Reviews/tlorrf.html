The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King

2003 | 3h 21m | PG-13

The Return of the King (2003), directed by Peter Jackson, is the epic conclusion to the *Lord of the Rings* trilogy. As Frodo and Sam approach Mount Doom to destroy the One Ring, Aragorn leads the forces of men against Sauron’s army in a final stand for Middle-earth. With sweeping battles, emotional farewells, and unforgettable moments, the film stars Elijah Wood, Viggo Mortensen, Ian McKellen, and Cate Blanchett. Winner of 11 Academy Awards, it remains one of the most celebrated fantasy films of all time.

“A cinematic triumph that closes the trilogy with grandeur.” – Empire

Parents Must Know

This film contains fantasy violence, intense battle sequences, and emotionally heavy themes. While there is no sexual content or profanity, the frightening imagery and war scenes may be unsettling for younger viewers. Recommended for ages 13 and up.

Sex & Nudity

No sexual content or nudity. A few romantic moments, including a kiss between characters, are brief and non-explicit.

Violence & Gore

Large-scale battles include sword fights, arrow strikes, and fantasy creatures. Some scenes show blood, severed limbs, and intense combat, but not excessively graphic.

Profanity

No profanity or offensive language is used throughout the film.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Characters are occasionally seen drinking ale or wine in celebratory scenes. No drug use is depicted.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Scenes include monstrous creatures, emotional deaths, and moments of despair. The tone is serious and dramatic, with some jump scares and dark imagery.

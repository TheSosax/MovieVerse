The Godfather: Part II

1974 | 3h 22m | R

Francis Ford Coppola's The Godfather: Part II is both a sequel and a prequel to the legendary original. This cinematic masterpiece explores the rise of young Vito Corleone while chronicling Michael's descent into darkness. A richly layered film that further cements the saga as one of cinema’s greatest achievements.

“An epic saga of power, family, and moral decay.” – Vincent Canby, The New York Times

“A flawless continuation that deepens the tragedy of the Corleone legacy.” – Roger Ebert

“One of the rare sequels that surpasses its predecessor.” – The Guardian

Parents Must Know

This film features intense violence including shootings, stabbings, and strangulations. The aftermath of a suicide and a graphic depiction of a deceased woman are also shown. There is frequent strong language, racial and ethnic slurs, and references to sex and male anatomy. Alcohol consumption and smoking are common. While artistically significant, this film is best suited for mature audiences.

Sex & Nudity

Mild sexual references and brief nudity. A brothel scene includes a deceased woman partially covered by a blanket.

Violence & Gore

Frequent mob-related killings, including shootings, strangulations, and a suicide aftermath. One woman is found dead in a bloody bed, handcuffed.

Profanity

Consistent strong language including f-words, ethnic and racial slurs.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Characters frequently drink wine, champagne, and smoke cigars. Heroin is briefly mentioned in discussions.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Suspenseful confrontations and scenes involving death or betrayal create a pervasive tone of dread and moral tension.

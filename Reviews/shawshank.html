The Shawshank Redemption

1994 | 2h 22m | R

The Shawshank Redemption (1994), directed by Frank Darabont and based on Stephen King's novella, follows Andy Dufresne, a banker wrongly convicted of murder, as he navigates life in Shawshank prison. Over two decades, he forms a deep friendship with fellow inmate Red and quietly plots a path to freedom. The film stars Tim Robbins and Morgan Freeman, and is widely regarded as one of the greatest movies of all time.

“A masterclass in storytelling and emotional depth.” – Empire

Parents Must Know

This film contains mature themes including prison violence, sexual assault references, and strong language. While nudity is minimal and non-sexual, the emotional intensity and dark tone make it unsuitable for children. Recommended for viewers aged 17 and older.

Content Rating (+18)

  • Sex & Nudity: mild

  • Profanity: severe
  • Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking: mild

Sex & Nudity

Brief kissing and implied sexual situations. References to sexual assault in prison are present but not graphically depicted.

Violence & Gore

Scenes include beatings, a suicide, and a shooting. Violence is impactful but not excessively graphic.

Profanity

Frequent use of strong language including the F-word, S-word, and other profanities throughout the film.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Characters drink beer and smoke cigarettes in several scenes. No drug use is shown.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Themes of injustice, isolation, and despair. Scenes of emotional breakdowns and prison brutality may be distressing to some viewers.

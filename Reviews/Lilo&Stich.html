Lilo & Stitch

2025 | 1h 48m | PG

Disney’s Lilo & Stitch (2025) is a live-action/CGI adaptation of the beloved 2002 animated film. Directed by Dean Fleischer Camp and starring Maia Kealoha as Lilo, the film reimagines the touching story of a lonely Hawaiian girl who finds friendship in an alien creature named Stitch. Exploring themes of family, identity, and grief, the movie blends heart, humor, and visual spectacle in a tropical setting.

“A warm and emotional gem that highlights heritage and family values.” – Parents.com

“Visually impressive and occasionally funny, though at times emotionally heavy.” – Common Sense Media

“The animated magic is lost in this dark and creatively lacking remake.” – New York Post

Parents Must Know

This PG-rated film includes cartoon-style action, mild peril, emotional themes, and light language. Children may be affected by scenes involving the loss of parents, child welfare issues, and scary alien chases. Language includes words like “stupid,” “hell,” and “Oh my God.” There are a few butt jokes and slapstick humor involving flatulence. Overall, it's suitable for children 6 and up, but may be emotionally intense for younger viewers.

Content Rating (PG / Ages 6+)

  • Sex & Nudity: none
  • Violence & Gore: mild
  • Profanity: Mild
  • Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking: none

Sex & Nudity

A kiss is shown in a photo during the credits. Some characters wear swimsuits; occasional mild body humor.

Violence & Gore

Frequent slapstick action including alien chases, taser blasts, crashing spaceships, and near-drownings. Tense moments but no blood or graphic violence.

Profanity

Mild language includes “stupid,” “hell,” and “Oh my God.” No harsh profanity.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

No drug use. A few background scenes may depict adults with drinks in hand, but nothing excessive or promoted.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Emotional themes of family loss, scenes involving potential abduction or danger, and intense alien pursuits. Some scenes may be too heavy for very young children.

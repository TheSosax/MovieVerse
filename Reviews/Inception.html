Inception

2010 | 2h 28m | PG-13

Inception is a masterpiece by Christopher Nolan that plays with the perception of reality and dreams. The film follows Dom Cobb (Leonardo DiCaprio), a thief who specializes in extracting secrets from the subconscious through dreams. But his most difficult mission will be implanting an idea in someone's mind without them realizing it. With a complex plot and stunning visual effects, Inception challenges logic and leaves the viewer questioning what is real.

  • “A dazzling puzzle of a movie.” – Roger Ebert

  • “Christopher Nolan’s masterpiece, brilliantly layered and unforgettable.” – Empire Magazine
  • “A breathtaking achievement in storytelling and visuals.” – The Guardian

    • Parents Must Know

    Parents must know: Inception is rated PG-13, meaning it contains moderate action sequences and violence. There are scenes of gunfire, explosions, and fights, though they are not excessively graphic. There is also an emotionally intense moment where a character jumps from a building. As for language, there are some mild profanities, but not in excess.

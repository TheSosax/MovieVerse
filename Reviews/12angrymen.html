12 Angry Men

1957 | 1h 36m | Approved

12 Angry Men (1957), directed by Sidney Lumet, is a gripping courtroom drama that unfolds almost entirely within a jury room. Twelve men must decide the fate of a teenage boy accused of murder. As tensions rise and biases surface, one juror challenges the others to reconsider the evidence. The film stars Henry Fonda, Lee J. Cobb, and Martin Balsam, and is celebrated for its powerful performances and timeless themes of justice and integrity.

Parents Must Know

This film contains no explicit content, but its mature themes—such as prejudice, justice, and moral responsibility—may be best appreciated by older viewers. The emotional intensity and ethical dilemmas make it a valuable watch for teens and adults alike.

Sex & Nudity

Violence & Gore

No physical violence is shown. The murder case is discussed in detail, but only verbally and without graphic imagery.

Profanity

No strong language. Occasional mild expressions like “damn” may be heard once.

Alcohol, Drugs & Smoking

Some jurors smoke cigarettes and pipes during deliberations. No drug use is depicted.

Frightening & Intense Scenes

Emotional confrontations and heated arguments may be intense for younger viewers. Themes of justice and prejudice are explored deeply.

The Dark Knight

2008 | 2h 32m | PG-13

The Dark Knight (2008) is a gripping and intense film that redefined superhero movies. With Christopher Nolan’s masterful direction and Heath Ledger’s legendary performance as The Joker, the movie dives into chaos, morality, and sacrifice as Batman struggles to protect Gotham from an unpredictable villain. Stunning cinematography, deep themes, and unforgettable performances make this a must-watch masterpiece.

Parents Must Know

The Dark Knight (2008) is rated PG-13 for intense sequences of violence and some menace(2). While the film contains no explicit sexual content, it does feature moderate violence, including fistfights, shootings, and explosions, though blood and gore are minimal(2). The Joker’s unpredictable and psychopathic nature may be too frightening for younger viewers(1). Parents should be aware that the film includes mild profanity and brief depictions of alcohol and smoking(2). Overall, it is best suited for teens and adults who can handle its dark themes and intense action.
